Municipalities of the National Capital Region (NCR)

Manila

The capital city known for its rich history and landmarks like Intramuros.

Quezon City

The largest city in NCR and a center for media and education.

Makati

The financial capital of the Philippines with major business districts.

Taguig

Home to Bonifacio Global City (BGC), a modern urban center.

Pasig

Known for Ortigas Center, one of the main business hubs.

Caloocan

A highly urbanized city divided into North and South areas.

Las Piñas

A city known for the historic Bamboo Organ and its residential character, serving as a gateway to the CALABARZON region

Malabon

A city known for its culinary heritage and ancestral homes, with a strong connection to Filipino traditions

Mandaluyong

A highly urbanized city and a key commercial and residential center in Metro Manila, sometimes called the "Tiger City of the Philippines"

Marikina

Known as the "Shoe Capital of the Philippines," known for its disciplined governance and quality of life

Muntinlupa

The southernmost city in Metro Manila, known for its residential areas and commercial centers

Paranaque

A highly urbanized city known for its commercial and industrial sectors.

Pasay

Known for its vibrant nightlife and the iconic Makati business district.

San Juan

A highly urbanized city known for its residential areas and its proximity to the central business district.

Valenzuela

A highly urbanized city known for its industrial and commercial sectors, and its proximity to the central business district.
